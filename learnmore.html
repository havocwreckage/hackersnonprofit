Wfae.org According to the report, officials counted nearly 1,700 homeless people in Charlotte-Mecklenburg on the night of January 31. That represents an increase of 13 percent from the one-night count last year. The report also found that the number of homeless people who stayed at shelters last year was down 17 percent. But the average amount of time people stayed in emergency shelters a year ago increased from 71 to 77 days.
